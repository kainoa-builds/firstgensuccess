From the founder

I built the thing I wish I'd had.

When I started college, I didn't know what office hours were really for, that internships had deadlines almost a year out, or that a resume needed more than a list of jobs. I learned most of it late, by accident, and usually after watching someone else already know.

I'm Kainoa Aqui — a first-gen grad from Kaimuki, Honolulu, Hawaiʻi. A descendant of Kamehameha. No network, no roadmap, no one to call who'd been through it. Just grit and a lot of figuring it out alone.

I went from a 2.66 GPA and no connections to internships at NSF, AWS, and Tesla — and eventually to a Product Manager role at IBM. Not because I was handed anything. Because I eventually found the playbook. Piece by piece, the hard way.

This site is that playbook, written down. Built for the student I was. Free. Growing every week. No gatekeeping.

— Kainoa Aqui First-gen grad & founder · IBM Product Manager · Kaimuki, Hawaiʻi
The mission

Why this site exists

The system isn't broken — it's just designed for people who already know the rules. First-gen students aren't behind because they're less capable. They're behind because they started the game without the rulebook everyone else grew up reading at home.

This site is the rulebook. The internship timelines. The email scripts. The salary conversations. The financial aid tips. The plain-English glossary. The guide for parents who want to help but don't know how. All of it — free, no login, no paywall, no "premium tier."

Built by a first-gen for first-gens. And growing every week.

What's coming next

Student stories

First-gen grads across industries sharing their paths, their mistakes, and what they wish they'd known. Real names, real journeys.

Templates library

Every email template you'll ever need — cold outreach, follow-up, thank-you notes, asking for a referral, negotiating an offer.

Campus resource finder

A searchable index of first-gen-specific programs, emergency funds, and support offices by school. Because every campus has them — and almost no one knows where they are.

Always free

This site will never charge for its core content. The students who need it most are also the ones who can afford it least. That's the whole point.
